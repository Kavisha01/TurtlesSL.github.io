Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery





Overview Of The Hatchery





Kosgoda, which is 3.8 kilometers (eight minutes) away from Ahungalle, is home to this turtle hatchery. The Perera family manages the project, which Dudley Perera started in 1981. Dudley is highly motivated and enthused about his cause, and he has training in marine animal protection. He enjoys imparting his knowledge to both residents and visitors.

This hatchery works hard to improve the hatching process and ensure the safe survival of young turtles after hatching in an effort to conserve and safeguard turtles. In addition, they tend to ill or damaged adult turtles before releasing them back into the water. Additionally, they hold courses and programs that educate both locals and foreign tourists.







More about Kosgoda

The foundation of the hatchery is the nighttime inspection of the beach for any eggs that have been placed or mother turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs. If any of these occurs, the eggs are sheltered and moved to the hatchery center to protect them from predator attacks or cruel treatment. The eggs are kept in a quiet environment until they hatch, which takes between 45 and 60 days. After hatching, the young turtles are released into the sea.

The volunteers patrol the beach at night to make sure that any mother turtles have proper conditions to lay eggs. They also carry the eggs back to the hatchery to make sure they hatch properly without any harm from predators and then release the baby sea turtles back into the ocean once properly hatched. They also take care of sea turtles with disabilities and treat the ones who have become weak due to fishing activities.

Tourists will be guided throughout the process of protecting and proper hatching of the sea turtles while visiting the hatchery. One can also visit the hatchery in the evening when the baby turtles are released into the sea as the risk for predators is minimum at this time and get the opportunity to release their baby sea turtle into the sea.
